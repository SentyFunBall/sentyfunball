Recap

Last year, I had the terrible wonderful idea to run a poll that would automatically figure out if a Stormworker was naughty or nice. Some of you may remember it. Last year, me and the elf managed to scrounge up 180 responses to the poll, which we were not prepared for at all.

It had us grading overtime, with over 900 manually graded text response questions. In addition, I set up an entire website where responders could compare themselves to the rest of Stormworks and download a fun certificate. People loved it.

So we did it again. We slightly tweaked the questions to be less biased, added some heavily biased questions & answers in there, and let the poll run hog wild. Me and the same elf from last year worked on grading responses through the magic of Google Forms and had all the data through the magic of Google Sheets.

Unlike last year, there were no questions that marked one as instantly naughty. If anything, having a joke response or a stupid name like "Big Dick Ben" made you instantly naughty, regardless of quiz score. Gradually, responses came in, and me and the elf worked hard grading. While unfortunately we had fewer responses than last year, this year still had some incredibly funny ones. Fortunately, it was much easier to grade and do everything this year, as we already had infrastructure in place from last year for grading, hosting, and websiting.

Once again, we closed it after a week, and after 120 responses, 840 manually graded answers, and 2040 total answers. If you missed it this year, sucks to suck. Thank you to everyone who participated, you make these polls fun.



Results summary

Insights

120 people took the quiz this year, and most of them were real responses. Still, 120 responses is a lot. Interestingly, the point distribution is slightly off from being a normal distribution. Interesting. The maximum possible points was 17, and no one got that this year, mostly cause there were no good bacon answers. The passing threshold was 12 points, and with that 60.5% are nice. If I make the passing number 11 instead, 79% are nice. This does mean that people got lower scores on average this year, perhaps due to the quiz, perhaps due to Stormworkers being less nice this year. 21 people got 12 points, 23 got 11.

Shoutout of course to the one person who got 0 points for a terrible joke response, and the one person who got 2 points, you probably deserved it.

Seeing how many people missed the questions on national loyalty and Lua was hilarious though. The loyalty one was rigged, the logic/Lua one was not. You all just don't wanna use Lua. You're wrong.


Questions

Do you think you're good or bad?

I like this question because it's purposefully worded vaguely. People can either decide if they were good at the game, or if they were good morally. Still, if you play Stormworks, the answer is no to both.

92 people (78%) answered this correctly, and 6 people claimed they are Deltars (I only added this option because Deltars allegedly answered the poll last year).

Have you ever stolen a vehicle?

This is straight forward and simple. Have you stolen a vehicle? If you have, that's very naughty. Some questioned the yambag callout, and that's because yambag steals vehicles people poured months of their lives into, claims them for himself, and refuses to say anything but "lol its a video game retard."

95 people (80%) have never stolen a vehicle. And those people are good. Shame on the other 20% of you.

What's your favorite propulsion method?

Stormworks has many different ways to make vehicles move, that's part of the games beauty. If you really wanted, you could build a nuclear powered car, or a jet boat, and who's going to stop you? Besides me, nobody! We left this question open, and 90 total people (76%) choose a correct answer. One person was doubly correct by answering "Pallet flying" and two people were doubly incorrect by answering "Diesel-electric", you know that doesn't work in Stormworks.

What Stormworks sub-community(s) are you apart of?

Stormworks has many offshoot communities for people to gather. I used to be part of many of them, but I left most of them throughout this year. You didn't need to know that, but it did contribute to the lower response count this year. Most people responded with communities I know of like bha, stoneworks, subworks, and EoS. For those who brought up a good offshoot, you got a point for this question. So probably like almost everyone.

Because it's a program of such nature, it is rather divisive. I really couldn't tell you what % think it's cheating or not, but I'd say <50% think so. Some of my favorite responses to this include:
